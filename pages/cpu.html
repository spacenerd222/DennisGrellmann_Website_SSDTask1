What does the CPU do?

The CPU is the most important part of a computer, responsible for performing all the computations and calculations necessary for the computer to function. It is often referred to as the "brain" of the computer, as it controls the flow of information and operations throughout the system. The CPU operates by retrieving instructions from the computer's memory, interpreting those instructions, and executing them. It does this by performing a series of logical and arithmetic operations on data, such as adding, subtracting, comparing, and moving data between memory locations. Overall, the CPU is a critical component of modern computing system. Without it, computers as we know them today would simply not exist.
